Even though the first electronic invoice, the digitized version of an invoice, was sent in 1965, many businesses still send their invoices via paper or PDF. The most common reason for this is that suppliers are comfortable with the current process and they usually get paid on time. However, businesses now have to process more transactions and going through a significant amount of invoices can be expensive, time-consuming, and error-prone. 

Digitizing invoices and sending e-invoice can bring significant benefits to all trade partners even though most think that changing to a new billing procedure is daunting and involves a lot of effort. In this blog post, we will be discussing what an electronic invoice is, why digitize invoices, and how to digitize invoices.

Although suppliers nowadays might send their invoices via PDF, that is not considered an electronic invoice. 

By definition, electronic invoicing, or e-invoicing, is a form of electronic billing that allows trade partners to **electronically and automatically process invoices**. The information stored in PDF files is not structured and therefore cannot be imported into any ERP or accounting software. In other words, for an invoice to be truly electronic, it must be able to enter, or be integrated, into a trade partner’s Accounts Payable (AP) system **without any manual data input**. Thus, an e-invoice is usually issued in Electronic Data Interchange (EDI), XML formats or any other common types of structured data. 

Digitizing invoices does more than just allowing businesses to go paperless and save some office space. Digitization of invoices that is well planned and executed can help businesses in a variety of ways.

### Reduce Costs Associated with Physical Invoices & Manual Processing

Organizations can enjoy substantial savings from digitizing invoices, whether it’s incoming or outgoing invoices. For outgoing invoices, you will no longer need to print out invoices, requiring ink and paper, and send them out in envelopes. For incoming invoices, costs related to manual processing invoices can be quite high once you have to deal with a lot of invoices. Digitizing invoices can then reduce or even eliminate these costs.

### Increase Efficiency of Invoice Processing & Save Time

Whether you are handling outgoing or incoming invoices, digitizing invoices can significantly help your organization improve the processing efficiency. The typical procedure of handling incoming invoices may include receiving the invoices, scanning them or entering the data into your systems manually, approving or rejecting invoices, submitting invoices for payments, and then archiving invoices and payment information. 

However, if your invoices are digitized, you will no longer have to manually input the information and since the data is structured, invoices can even be processed and approved automatically by AP software. 

### Reduce Human Errors and Avoid Payment Delays

Employees can suffer from stress and fatigue, resulting in human errors. In addition, invoices might sometimes get omitted or processed late, which could lead to payment delays. Going digital can then speed up and automate the process to reduce human errors and avoid payment delays. 

There are some other advantages of digitizing invoices such as ease of searching for information, increased durability, ability for business intelligence, etc. Although many might not see how digitizing invoices, or e-invoicing, can create values for their organizations at first sight, many businesses have certainly adopted it and greatly benefited from it. 

Manual data entry was the main means of invoice digitization. Staff from the AP department receive invoices and enter the information into the AP software. In order to replace manual data entry, some may use optical character recognition (OCR) to extract data from invoices. However, using OCR itself is not enough since the results are simply unorganized texts that cannot be directly imported into other software.
